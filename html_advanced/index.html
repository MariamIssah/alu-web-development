Climate-smart agriculture Importance of Cover crops

How does it work

Crop planning and rotation involve strategically planting different crops in the same area across different seasons or years to maintain soil health, reduce pests and diseases, and improve crop yield. Greenhouse production allows for the controlled growth of crops in a regulated environment, optimizing factors such as temperature, humidity, light, and ventilation to enhance growth and productivity.

How does it work

Climate-smart agriculture focuses on increasing productivity and resilience to climate change while reducing greenhouse gas emissions through practices like drought-resistant crop varieties, improved irrigation, and reduced tillage. Cover crops, grown primarily to cover the soil rather than for harvest, play a vital role in sustainable farming by protecting soil from erosion, improving soil health and fertility, and controlling weeds and pests.

How does it work

Harvest and handling involve the processes of gathering mature crops and managing them post-harvest to maintain quality and preserve freshness. Irrigation refers to the controlled application of water to crops to supplement rainfall and ensure optimal growth and yield. Propagation involves the reproduction of plants through methods such as seed germination, cuttings, or grafting to create new plants for cultivation. These practices are essential components of agricultural management, ensuring efficient crop production, quality control, and sustainable farming practices.

How does it work

Season extension techniques, such as using greenhouses or high tunnels, prolong the growing season, ensuring continuous crop production beyond typical outdoor periods. Seed saving involves preserving seeds from mature plants to maintain biodiversity and reduce reliance on commercial seeds. Soil health, vital for plant growth, is enhanced through practices like crop rotation and cover cropping, ensuring fertile, resilient soil for sustainable agriculture.
